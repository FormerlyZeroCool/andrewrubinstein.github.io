An Introduction to me

“Success consists of moving from failure to failure without a loss of enthusiasm.”
-Often attributed to Winston Churchill
This is a quote that continues to help me to this day.

I'm 28 now, and I spent my early 20's working for an Israeli software company that developed a specialized system for the Diamond industry, that manages inventory, accounting, and some customer relationship management or CRM tasks.

The company gave me responsibilities that included Microsoft SQL business intelligence report development, sales, and implementation of the software for new clients; I learned all of this on the job.

I traveled to countries around the world where I worked with clients to setup, troubleshoot the software, and build custom SSRS reports, keeping up with my tasks in the different time zones.
I learned and I got to travel the world.

Yet one piece was missing. Although I had honed many skills and built rapport with customers, I wanted education. So, I decided I was ready to return to an academic environment.
I left my position in 2018 and enrolled at the Borough of Manhattan Community College.
During my time as a student at The Borough of Manhattan Community College(BMCC) I continued to build on my knowledge, gained new friends,
and through that the opportunity to pursue an education and Columbia Univerity's School of General Studies
My time at Columbia so far has been a humbling one forcing me to double down on my efforts, and work harder than ever before in an academic environment.
It has been exactly what I hoped to find, a real challenge, and something that keeps me working to improve myself
I have also been working in both the professional and academic worlds during this time Assistant Teaching at BMCC, and freelancing as a developer, and SSRS reporter for former clients
In addition to all this I try to make as much time as I can for my family, and hobby projects, like a framework I am building to build interpreters
and transpilers by defining a set of tokens that define the words of the language via regex, or dictionary, a context free grammar to define the syntax of a language
and an attribute grammar to define the semantic rules of a given language to be interpreted or transpiled.
All of these are defined in their own language, and are bootstrapped using one another.
